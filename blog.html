Does Aromatherapy Work for Sleep? Maybe. Here’s What We Know

Posted on September 1, 2024 | 18 mins read

Aromatherapy is widely used to improve sleep, but there’s not much evidence it’s actually effective. Sleep experts recommend focusing on sleep hygiene first.

Understanding Aromatherapy

Aromatherapy involves using essential oils and other aromatic compounds to enhance physical and emotional well-being. Lavender, chamomile, and valerian are some of the most common essential oils used to promote relaxation and sleep.

The Science Behind Aromatherapy

While many people swear by aromatherapy for better sleep, the scientific evidence supporting its efficacy is limited. Some studies suggest that essential oils like lavender can help reduce anxiety and improve sleep quality, but the results are not conclusive.

It’s important to approach aromatherapy with realistic expectations. While it may help some individuals relax and prepare for sleep, it’s not a guaranteed solution for sleep problems.

Focus on Sleep Hygiene

Experts agree that good sleep hygiene is the key to improving sleep quality. This includes maintaining a regular sleep schedule, creating a comfortable sleep environment, and avoiding stimulants like caffeine before bed.

Incorporating aromatherapy into your bedtime routine can be a soothing addition, but it should not replace evidence-based sleep practices.
